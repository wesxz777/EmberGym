# Results and Discussion

   After collecting data and analyzing them, the researchers found the following.

### **System Usability Assessment**

   The five usability dimensions were evaluated based on responses from 15 Grade 11 ICT students at the University of Makati. Table 1 presents the weighted mean (WM), standard deviation (SD), and interpretation for each usability dimension assessed in the study (see Table 1).

Table 1
Usability Dimensions - Descriptive Statistics

| Usability Dimension | Weighted Mean (WM) | Standard Deviation (SD) | Interpretation |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---|
| Interaction Quality | 4.44 | 0.37 | Very High |
| Functional Stability | 4.36 | 0.39 | Very High |
| Reliability | 4.25 | 0.51 | Very High |
| Security | 4.17 | 0.58 | High |
| Flexibility | 4.00 | 0.63 | High |
| Overall Usability | 4.24 | 0.50 | Very High |

   The overall usability rating of 4.24 indicates that Grade 11 ICT students perceived the EmberGym website as having "Very High" usability. This finding aligns with the Technology Acceptance Model proposed by Davis (1989), which emphasizes that perceived ease of use is a primary driver of technology adoption among users. Similarly, Nielsen (1993) established usability principles that highlight the importance of clear, intuitive system design—a strength evident in the high ratings for Interaction Quality (4.44) and Functional Stability (4.36) obtained in this study.

   *Interaction quality and functional stability.* The highest-rated dimension was Interaction Quality with a weighted mean of 4.44 (SD=0.37), indicating that students found the website interface clear, well-organized, and easy to navigate. The low standard deviation suggests strong consensus among respondents about interface effectiveness. Functional Stability also received a very high rating of 4.36 (SD=0.39), demonstrating that students perceived the system as reliable and consistent during use. These findings are consistent with Brooke's (1996) System Usability Scale research, which emphasizes that users value dependable systems that perform consistently across sessions.

   *Security and reliability.* Security and Reliability dimensions both received ratings of 4.17 and 4.25 respectively, indicating "Very High" and "High" interpretations. Students demonstrated confidence in the system's data protection measures and the accuracy of transaction processing. This aligns with Patel and Kumar (2021), who found that security perceptions significantly influence user trust in online transaction systems.

   *Flexibility in cross-device functionality.* The Flexibility dimension received the lowest usability rating of 4.00 (SD=0.63), still interpreted as "High" but suggesting opportunities for improvement. This dimension addressed system adaptability across different devices and network conditions. The higher standard deviation (0.63) indicates that student experiences with cross-device functionality varied considerably, possibly reflecting differences in the devices they used (smartphone, tablet, laptop) or their home network conditions. Eze et al. (2022) emphasize that modern web applications must function seamlessly across diverse devices and network conditions; the lower rating here suggests that optimization for mobile devices and variable network speeds should be a future development priority.

### **Booking Efficiency Assessment**

   The three booking efficiency dimensions were similarly evaluated based on student responses. Table 2 presents the weighted mean, standard deviation, and interpretation results for booking efficiency-related perceptions (see Table 2).

Table 2
Booking Efficiency Dimensions - Descriptive Statistics

| Efficiency Dimension | Weighted Mean (WM) | Standard Deviation (SD) | Interpretation |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---|
| Convenience | 4.33 | 0.41 | Very High |
| Speed | 4.17 | 0.58 | High |
| Accuracy | 4.13 | 0.64 | High |
| Overall Booking Efficiency | 4.21 | 0.54 | Very High |

   The overall booking efficiency rating of 4.21 demonstrates that students perceived the EmberGym booking system as "Very High" in efficiency. This strong performance is particularly important, as DeLone and McLean (2003) emphasize in their Information Systems Success Model that efficiency in transaction processing directly contributes to user satisfaction and system success. Additionally, Castañeda and Camacho (2022) found that booking efficiency directly influences user satisfaction and repeat usage among technology users.

   *Convenience as a key strength.* The Convenience dimension received the highest efficiency rating of 4.33 (SD=0.41), indicating that students found the booking process easy to complete, well-integrated with their schedules, and accessible. The consistency of ratings (low SD of 0.41) shows broad agreement on this system strength. Patel and Kumar (2021) similarly identified convenience in online booking systems as a critical success factor that significantly influences user satisfaction. This finding suggests that the EmberGym system successfully integrates booking functionality into student workflows with minimal friction.

   *Speed and accuracy considerations.* Speed (WM=4.17, SD=0.58) and Accuracy (WM=4.13, SD=0.64) dimensions showed slightly lower ratings compared to Convenience, though both remained in the "High" interpretation category. Students perceived the system as adequately fast for typical use, but qualitative feedback revealed that some experienced occasional delays during peak usage times. Regarding accuracy, the 4.13 rating indicates students were generally confident that their bookings were recorded correctly—a crucial trust factor in financial transactions. However, the higher standard deviation (0.64) suggests that some respondents experienced errors or had lower confidence in data accuracy, potentially warranting attention to error-handling and confirmation processes in future system updates.

## Discussion

### Interpretation of Usability Findings

The very high usability rating (4.24) indicates that the EmberGym website successfully meets the ISO/IEC 25010 quality standards for interaction quality and functional stability. According to Davis's (1989) Technology Acceptance Model (TAM), perceived ease of use—directly reflected in the high Interaction Quality rating—is a primary driver of technology adoption. The 4.44 rating for Interaction Quality suggests that students found minimal barriers to using the system, which supports continued adoption among the target user population.

The Functional Stability dimension (4.36) reflects students' confidence in the system's reliability and consistency, aligning with findings from Brooke's (1996) System Usability Scale research, which emphasizes that users value dependable systems that perform consistently across sessions.

However, the Flexibility dimension (4.00), while still rated as "High," presents an important consideration. Eze et al. (2022) note that modern web applications must function seamlessly across diverse devices and network conditions. The lower rating here—combined with qualitative feedback about cross-device issues—suggests that optimization for mobile devices and variable network speeds should be a future development priority. The standard deviation of 0.63 indicates that student experiences with cross-device functionality varied considerably, possibly reflecting differences in the devices they used (smartphone, tablet, laptop) or their home network conditions.

### Interpretation of Booking Efficiency Findings

The very high booking efficiency rating (4.21) demonstrates that the EmberGym system effectively serves its primary function: enabling students to book fitness facility services quickly, accurately, and conveniently. According to DeLone and McLean's (2003) IS Success Model, efficiency in transaction processing directly contributes to user satisfaction and system success.

The Convenience dimension (4.33) is particularly noteworthy. Patel and Kumar (2021) emphasize that convenience in online booking systems significantly influences user satisfaction and repeat usage. The high rating here suggests that students experienced minimal friction in the booking workflow—a critical success factor for e-commerce and service-booking systems. The low standard deviation (0.41) indicates that nearly all respondents agreed on this system strength.

The Speed and Accuracy dimensions, while still rated as "High" (4.17 and 4.13 respectively), provide valuable insights. Students perceived the system as adequately fast, but some noted occasional delays. Qualitative feedback about bot response speed suggests that while the booking system itself is responsive, the customer support infrastructure could be enhanced. Regarding accuracy, the 4.13 rating indicates students were generally confident that their bookings were recorded correctly—a crucial trust factor in financial transactions. However, the higher standard deviation (0.64) suggests some experienced errors or had lower confidence, potentially warranting attention to error-handling and confirmation processes.

### Synthesis: Usability and Booking Efficiency Relationship

While the research design was primarily descriptive, the parallel assessment of usability and efficiency dimensions reveals an interesting pattern: dimensions that received the highest usability ratings (Interaction Quality and Functional Stability) correspond to the high overall booking efficiency rating. This suggests that—as expected from user experience principles—superior interface design and system reliability create conditions where users can complete booking tasks efficiently (Alsaif & Makarim, 2021).

Conversely, the Flexibility dimension's lower rating correlates with the slightly lower Speed and Accuracy ratings, suggesting that device and network constraints may impact both perceived usability and efficiency. This interdependence highlights the importance of comprehensive system optimization across all quality dimensions.

### Alignment with Research Objectives

The findings directly address the research objectives:

1. **Objective 1 - Perceived Usability Level**: Students rated the EmberGym system as having "Very High" usability (WM=4.24), with particular strengths in interface clarity and system reliability. This demonstrates that the system successfully achieves its design goal of being user-friendly for Grade 11 students.

2. **Objective 2 - Booking Efficiency Level**: Students perceived booking efficiency as "Very High" (WM=4.21), with convenience as the standout strength. The system effectively enables students to schedule fitness bookings with minimal effort and delays.

3. **Objective 3 - System Performance**: The consistency between high usability and high booking efficiency ratings suggests that the EmberGym system's design effectively translates user-friendly interfaces into efficient transaction processing—a key indicator of overall system quality.

## Conclusions

Based on the analysis of data collected from 15 Grade 11 ICT students at the University of Makati, the following conclusions are drawn:

1. **The EmberGym website exhibits Very High usability** across most dimensions (Interaction Quality: 4.44, Functional Stability: 4.36, Reliability: 4.25), indicating that the system meets international quality standards (ISO/IEC 25010) for user interface design and system dependability. Grade 11 ICT students, even as first-time users, found the system intuitive and straightforward to use.

2. **The EmberGym booking system demonstrates Very High efficiency**, with students rating the overall experience at 4.21. The particularly high Convenience rating (4.33) shows that the booking process is well-integrated with student workflows and requires minimal effort to complete.

3. **Cross-device functionality and support responsiveness present optimization opportunities**. While not critical issues, the lower Flexibility dimension (4.00) and qualitative feedback about bot response speed indicate specific areas where targeted improvements could further enhance the student experience.

4. **Overall, the EmberGym system successfully serves its intended purpose** of providing a usable, efficient platform for fitness facility booking. The system shows strong potential for adoption among the Grade 11 ICT student population and likely among other student groups with similar technology comfort levels.

## Recommendations

Based on the findings and conclusions, the following recommendations are proposed:

### For System Development:

1. **Enhance Cross-Device Responsiveness**
   - Conduct comprehensive testing across Android, iOS, and various desktop browsers to ensure consistent user experience
   - Optimize for slower network speeds and poor connectivity scenarios, as some students may access the system from areas with limited bandwidth
   - Rationale: The Flexibility dimension's lower rating suggests this area deserves targeted attention to achieve consistent "Very High" ratings across all quality dimensions

2. **Improve Customer Support Bot Responsiveness**
   - Analyze current bot response delays and optimize the support system architecture
   - Consider implementing faster response times or human escalation thresholds for common inquiries
   - Rationale: Qualitative feedback identified bot speed as a specific concern; addressing this could enhance overall user satisfaction

3. **Maintain Interface and Stability Strengths**
   - Continue applying the design principles that earned high Interaction Quality and Functional Stability ratings
   - Document these design patterns as standards for future feature development
   - Rationale: These dimensions represent system strengths that differentiate EmberGym from potential competitors

### For Future Research:

1. **Expand the Study Scope**
   - Conduct similar assessments with other student populations (different grades, universities) to verify findings' generalizability
   - Include non-ICT students to understand usability across diverse technical backgrounds
   - Rationale: The current study's 15-student sample provides valuable directional insights; larger, more diverse samples would strengthen conclusions

2. **Investigate Device-Specific Experiences**
   - Disaggregate data by device type (smartphone vs. desktop) to identify platform-specific issues
   - Analyze how network speed correlates with perceived booking efficiency
   - Rationale: Understanding platform-specific challenges could guide targeted optimization efforts

3. **Longitudinal Assessment**
   - Track user satisfaction over time as students gain experience with the system
   - Measure whether initial high usability ratings predict long-term adoption and usage frequency
   - Rationale: Kauer et al. (2017) note that perceived usability may evolve as users gain familiarity with systems; longitudinal studies capture these dynamics

### For Implementation Priority:

- **High Priority**: Address bot response speed and cross-device functionality issues (tied to qualitative feedback)
- **Medium Priority**: Conduct expanded research to verify findings with larger, more diverse samples
- **Ongoing**: Continue monitoring user feedback and maintaining the interface design standards that drive high usability ratings
