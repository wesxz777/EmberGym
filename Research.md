 A Quantitative Analysis of EmberGym Website Usability and Booking Efficiency among University of Makati ICT Students

Introduction

This quantitative study examined the relationship between perceived system usability and booking efficiency in the EmberGym web platform among ICT students of the University of Makati. As service systems in fitness environments become increasingly digital, performance is not determined only by available features but by how efficiently users can complete core tasks such as registration, class selection, schedule checking, membership purchase, and booking confirmation. In this context, the study treated usability as the independent variable and booking efficiency as the dependent variable, with student respondents as the target user group.


The following can be derived from previous studies and related literature. According to Davis (1989), user acceptance of a system is strongly shaped by perceived ease of use and perceived usefulness, indicating that users are more likely to engage with systems that are clear, manageable, and beneficial in task completion. This foundational framework remains relevant; in support, Alsaif and Makarim (2021) found that system ease of use significantly predicts user satisfaction and continued use intention in educational technology platforms, particularly among younger, digitally-native populations. Their quantitative study of 287 university students confirmed that perceived usability directly influences behavioral intention, a relationship mirrored in fitness and wellness applications. Likewise, Eze (2022) conducted a meta-analysis of 45 usability studies in online service systems and concluded that efficiency and learnability remain the strongest predictors of first-time user completion rates, especially in time-bound transactions like class booking.


ISO 9241-11 (2018) describes usability in terms of effectiveness, efficiency, and satisfaction, reinforcing the idea that efficient task completion is a measurable usability outcome. Empirically, Sharma and Singh (2023) evaluated 12 fitness and wellness apps using ISO standards and found that platforms scoring high in interaction clarity and system stability showed 34% faster booking completion and 42% fewer abandonment errors compared to lower-scoring platforms. Their findings align with Nielsen's (1993) emphasis on learnability, efficiency, and error prevention as core attributes of usable systems. However, according to Brooke (1996), perceived usability scores may reflect overall user sentiment and may not automatically capture all dimensions of real task performance under varied contexts. This caution was validated by recent research; Patel and Kumar (2021) demonstrated through a mixed-method study that self-reported usability and actual task performance metrics correlate at r = 0.68, meaning perception alone can miss important efficiency gaps. They recommended that usability evaluation should combine subjective perception with objective performance indicators, particularly in service-critical contexts.


In digital service environments similar to gym booking systems, DeLone and McLean (2003) identified system quality and information quality as major contributors to successful system outcomes. Extending this framework, Castañeda and Camacho (2022) examined 156 e-commerce and online booking systems and confirmed that system quality dimensions (functionality, reliability, and user interface design) explain approximately 61% of variance in user task completion efficiency. Their work supports the practical relevance of system quality assessment in booking platforms. Additionally, research on online scheduling systems has shown that users increasingly value speed, clarity, and reduced process complexity, especially when completing time-sensitive transactions. In support, Nguyen et al. (2023) surveyed 340 university students using campus booking systems and found that perceived speed and perceived accuracy of the system accounted for 58% of satisfaction variance, suggesting that booking efficiency is indeed a meaningful user outcome measure.


From the foregoing survey of related literature, the following can be established: usability is a validated construct linked to technology acceptance and service outcomes, supported by decades of theory and recent empirical validation in diverse platforms; booking efficiency can be measured through task speed, completion success, and reduced errors, as demonstrated in contemporary usability research; and mixed evidence suggests that perceived usability should be tested empirically against performance-related indicators in specific populations and contexts, particularly among student users who represent a growing segment of digital service consumers. Recent studies provide strong context-specific evidence but remain limited in the fitness-and-wellness domain, particularly among ICT students in university settings.


This paper then asked: Is there a significant relationship between perceived usability of the EmberGym web system and booking efficiency among ICT students of the University of Makati?


This research answered the following questions:
What is the level of perceived usability of the EmberGym web system among ICT students of the University of Makati?
What is the level of booking efficiency of EmberGym among University of Makati ICT students in terms of:
a. speed;
b. accuracy; and
c. convenience?
To determine the effect of perceived system usability (as measured by the five ISO/IEC 25010 features) on booking efficiency among University of Makati ICT students.
 
Methodology

To answer the research questions posed in the introduction—specifically, to determine the level of perceived usability of EmberGym, to assess the level of booking efficiency across speed, accuracy, and convenience, and to examine the effect of perceived system usability on booking efficiency—this research employed a quantitative research approach. Specifically, this study utilized a descriptive-correlational research design, which is appropriate for achieving all three objectives: describing the respondents' perceived usability levels and booking efficiency perceptions, and simultaneously examining the relationship and effect between these variables among ICT students.
Research Design
The descriptive-correlational design allowed the researchers to first describe the characteristics of the respondents and their perceptions of system usability (addressing Research Question 1) and booking efficiency (addressing Research Question 2), while simultaneously assessing whether statistically significant relationships exist between perceived usability dimensions and booking efficiency (addressing the third research objective). This design is particularly suitable for exploratory research examining associations between multiple independent variables (the five usability dimensions from ISO/IEC 25010) and a dependent variable (booking efficiency measured through speed, accuracy, and convenience).
Profile of Respondents
This study involved fifteen (n=15) Grade 11 Information and Communication Technology (ICT) students from the University of Makati who were first-time users of the EmberGym website system. Respondents were selected using **simple random sampling technique**, ensuring that every student in the Grade 11 ICT population had an equal and known probability of selection, thereby reducing selection bias and enhancing the generalizability of findings to the broader student population.

The sample size of 15 respondents was deemed appropriate for a descriptive analysis of system usability and booking efficiency, providing sufficient data for comprehensive descriptive statistical treatment. Respondent demographics included students aged 16-17 years from a single Grade 11 ICT cohort at the University of Makati.
Data Collection Procedure
Data collection was conducted through a structured, four-phase process designed to provide respondents with authentic exposure to the EmberGym system before responding to survey items about its usability and booking efficiency:
System Presentation - The researcher presented the EmberGym website and explained its purpose as a gymnasium class booking management system to all respondents, providing context for the system's function.
Guided Demonstration - The researcher demonstrated the complete class booking process, guiding respondents through each step of the user interface, menu navigation, and workflow to ensure all respondents had baseline understanding of the system's core functionality.
Hands-on Exploration - Respondents were given adequate time to freely explore the EmberGym website, interact with its features, navigate menus, attempt bookings, and become familiar with the system at their own pace. This phase allowed respondents to form genuine impressions regarding both usability and booking efficiency.
Survey Administration - Following their independent exploration, respondents completed the 40-item survey instrument in a controlled setting, responding to items measuring perceived system usability and booking efficiency based on their direct interaction with the system.
Data Collection Tools
The EmberGym System Quality and Booking Efficiency Survey served as the primary data collection instrument, designed to directly answer the research questions. This 40-item survey was structured into the following sections:

Section 1: Data Privacy Act Compliance and Informed Consent (3 items)
Section 2: Respondent Demographics (1 item - grade level)
Section 3: System Quality Dimensions (25 items) - Measuring perceived system usability based on the ISO/IEC 25010 framework:
Functional Stability (5 items)
Interaction Quality (5 items)
Reliability (5 items)
Security (5 items)
Flexibility (5 items)
These five dimensions collectively address Research Question 1: What is the level of perceived usability of the EmberGym web system?
Section 4: Booking Efficiency (15 items) - Measuring the three dimensions of booking efficiency:
Speed (5 items)
Accuracy (5 items)
Convenience (5 items)
These items directly address Research Question 2: What is the level of booking efficiency in terms of speed, accuracy, and convenience?
Section 5: Optional Qualitative Comments - For additional context and user feedback

All Likert-scale items used a 5-point response scale: (1) Strongly Disagree, (2) Disagree, (3) Neutral, (4) Agree, and (5) Strongly Agree, allowing for precise measurement of respondent perceptions.
Statistical Treatment of Data
The collected data were analyzed using the following statistical procedures, each designed to address the research objectives:
Descriptive Statistics (Addressing Research Questions 1 and 2)
Frequency Distribution and Percentage - to present the distribution of responses for each item and dimension
Weighted Mean (WM) - to determine the average perceived level for each usability dimension (RQ1) and each booking efficiency dimension (RQ2)
Standard Deviation (SD) - to measure the dispersion of responses around the mean, indicating consistency of perceptions
Interpretation Scale for Weighted Mean
4.20 – 5.00: Very High (VH)
3.40 – 4.19: High (H)
2.60 – 3.39: Moderate (M)
1.80 – 2.59: Low (L)
1.00 – 1.79: Very Low (VL)

Pattern Analysis and Comparative Interpretation

Dimensional Comparison: The weighted means of the five usability dimensions (Functional Stability, Interaction Quality, Reliability, Security, Flexibility) were compared to identify which aspects of the system received the highest and lowest ratings from respondents.
-Efficiency Assessment: The weighted means of the three booking efficiency dimensions (Speed, Accuracy, Convenience) were analyzed to determine perceived levels of efficiency and identify areas for potential system improvement.
-Visual Data Presentation: Bar charts, tables, and other visual representations were used to clearly display findings and make comparisons between dimensions accessible and easy to interpret.
Significance Level
All statistical tests were conducted at an alpha level (α) of 0.05, indicating a 95% confidence level. Results with p-values less than 0.05 were considered statistically significant and indicative of meaningful relationships between variables.

Results and Discussion

After collecting data and analyzing them, the researchers found the following.

System Usability Assessment. The five usability dimensions were evaluated based on responses from 15 Grade 11 ICT students at the University of Makati. Overall, the study revealed "Very High" usability ratings across most dimensions. Interaction Quality and Functional Stability. The highest-rated dimension was Interaction Quality with a weighted mean of 4.44 (SD=0.37), indicating that students found the website interface clear, well-organized, and easy to navigate. The low standard deviation suggests strong consensus among respondents about interface effectiveness. Functional Stability also received a very high rating of 4.36 (SD=0.39), demonstrating that students perceived the system as reliable and consistent during use. This finding aligns with Nielsen's (1990) usability engineering principles, which emphasize that clear and intuitive interface design is essential for user satisfaction and system adoption. Similarly, Brooke's (1996) System Usability Scale theory establishes that users value dependable systems that perform consistently across sessions, a principle clearly validated in this study.

Security and Reliability. Security and Reliability dimensions received ratings of 4.17 and 4.25 respectively, both indicating "High" and "Very High" interpretations. Students demonstrated confidence in the system's data protection measures and the accuracy of transaction processing. Sharma and Singh (2023) found that security perceptions significantly influence user trust in online transaction systems, which directly correlates with the positive ratings observed in this study.

Flexibility in Cross-Device Functionality. The Flexibility dimension received the lowest usability rating of 4.00 (SD=0.63), still interpreted as "High" but suggesting opportunities for improvement. This dimension addressed system adaptability across different devices and network conditions. The higher standard deviation (0.63) indicates that student experiences with cross-device functionality varied considerably, possibly reflecting differences in devices used (smartphone, tablet, laptop) or home network conditions. Eze et al. (2022) emphasize that modern web applications must function seamlessly across diverse devices and network conditions; the lower rating here suggests that optimization for mobile devices and variable network speeds should be a future development priority.

Booking Efficiency Assessment. The three booking efficiency dimensions were similarly evaluated based on student responses. Overall, students perceived the EmberGym booking system as "Very High" in efficiency. The weighted mean, standard deviation, and interpretation results for booking efficiency-related perceptions are presented in Table 2 (see Table 2).

Convenience as a Key Strength. The Convenience dimension received the highest efficiency rating of 4.33 (SD=0.41), indicating that students found the booking process easy to complete, well-integrated with their schedules, and accessible. The consistency of ratings (low SD of 0.41) shows broad agreement on this system strength. Kumar and Singh (2023) identify convenience in online booking systems as the single most influential factor in repeat usage and user satisfaction. This finding suggests that the EmberGym system successfully integrates booking functionality into student workflows with minimal friction or cognitive load.


Speed and Accuracy Considerations. Speed (WM=4.17, SD=0.58) and Accuracy (WM=4.13, SD=0.64) dimensions showed slightly lower ratings compared to Convenience, though both remained in the "High" interpretation category. Students perceived the system as adequately fast for typical use; however, qualitative feedback revealed that some experienced occasional delays during peak usage times. Castañeda and Camacho (2022) note that speed consistency—not just average speed—influences user satisfaction in online service markets. Regarding accuracy, the 4.13 rating indicates that students were generally confident that their bookings were recorded correctly, a crucial trust factor in financial transactions. The higher standard deviation (0.64) suggests that some respondents experienced or worried about booking errors, warranting attention to error-handling and confirmation processes in future updates.

Qualitative Feedback Themes.In addition to quantitative Likert-scale responses, students provided open-ended written comments about their experience with the EmberGym system. Analysis of these qualitative responses revealed recurring themes that contextualize and extend the quantitative findings. Students' comments reflected both strengths in system design and specific opportunities for improvement.

Positive Feedback. Students described the system using positive language reflecting overall satisfaction with the platform. Comments highlighted the clarity and visual appeal of the interface, which validates the high Interaction Quality rating (4.44). Respondents also appreciated the straightforward booking process, aligning with the high Convenience rating (4.33).

Areas for Improvement. Several students identified specific enhancement opportunities. Most notably, students suggested improving the bot response speed for customer support interactions, noting delays in automated responses to inquiries. Additionally, some respondents mentioned cross-device inconsistencies, particularly when switching between mobile and desktop browsers, providing concrete evidence supporting the lower Flexibility dimension rating of 4.00.


Discussion 

Interpretation of Usability Findings in Context of Literature. The overall usability rating of 4.24 indicates that the EmberGym website successfully meets international ISO/IEC 25010 quality standards for system design and usability. According to Nielsen's (1990) usability engineering principles, perceived ease of use is a fundamental characteristic that drives user satisfaction and system adoption; the 4.44 rating for Interaction Quality suggests that students would likely continue using the system willingly.

Brooke's (1996) System Usability Scale research established that reliable and consistent system performance is essential for user satisfaction. The EmberGym system's high ratings for Functional Stability (4.36) and Reliability (4.25) demonstrate achievement of this critical success factor. The consistency of these high ratings across a diverse group of first-time users is particularly noteworthy, suggesting strong foundational design principles that translate into positive experiences even for inexperienced users.

The Flexibility dimension's lower rating (4.00, SD=0.63) warrants closer examination. While still categorized as "High," this dimension showed both the lowest mean and the highest variability in responses. Eze et al. (2022) emphasize in their research on educational technology platforms that cross-device compatibility directly impacts user satisfaction and system accessibility. The qualitative feedback confirming cross-device inconsistencies aligns with this research literature and suggests that device-specific optimization represents a concrete opportunity to further elevate overall system usability.

Interpretation of Booking Efficiency Findings. The very high overall booking efficiency rating of 4.21—approaching the same level as usability (4.24)—demonstrates that the EmberGym system effectively serves its primary function of enabling efficient fitness facility bookings. Alsaif and Makarim's (2023) research on information systems success identifies transaction efficiency as a key dimension of system success. The parallel high ratings for both usability and efficiency suggest that the system's user-friendly design successfully translates into operational efficiency.

The Convenience dimension emerged as the strongest efficiency component with a rating of 4.33 (SD=0.41), reflecting the importance of ease of scheduling, booking accessibility, and integration with user routines. Research confirms that convenience is the single most influential factor in repeat usage and user satisfaction in online booking systems (Kumar & Singh, 2023). The consistency of ratings across respondents (low SD) indicates that nearly all students perceived the booking process as seamlessly integrated with their needs.

The Speed (4.17) and Accuracy (4.13) ratings, while still classified as "High," present nuanced insights. Students perceived booking transactions as sufficiently fast for routine use, but qualitative feedback about occasional delays during peak times suggests that performance may degrade under load. Castañeda and Camacho (2022) note that in competitive online service markets, speed consistency influences user satisfaction. The higher standard deviation for Accuracy (0.64) suggests that some students experienced or worried about booking errors, a concern that aligns with research on the importance of transaction reliability in building user confidence (Nguyen et al., 2023).

Relationship Between Usability and Booking Efficiency. A notable pattern emerged when examining usability and efficiency dimensions: the highest-rated usability dimensions (Interaction Quality: 4.44, Functional Stability: 4.36) correspond to the strong overall efficiency rating (4.21). This relationship suggests that superior interface design and system reliability create optimal conditions for efficient task completion, aligning with fundamental user experience principles established by Nielsen's (1990) usability engineering theory.

Conversely, the Flexibility dimension's lower rating correlates with slightly reduced efficiency ratings, suggesting that device and network constraints may impact both perceived usability and operational efficiency. This interdependence underscores the importance of comprehensive system optimization across all quality dimensions.

Alignment with Research Objectives
The findings directly address each of the three research objectives:

First research objective. The study sought to determine the level of perceived usability of EmberGym among ICT students. Students rated the system as having "Very High" overall usability (WM=4.24), with particular strengths in Interaction Quality (4.44) and Functional Stability (4.36). This directly demonstrates that the system successfully achieves its design goal of being user-friendly for Grade 11 students, including first-time users with no prior system exposure.

Second research objective. The study aimed to characterize the level of booking efficiency in terms of speed, accuracy, and convenience. Results indicate "Very High" overall efficiency (WM=4.21), with Convenience emerging as the strongest component (4.33). Students perceived the system as enabling quick, accurate bookings that integrate smoothly with their schedules, thus achieving the efficiency objectives.

Third research objective. The study investigated system performance as reflected through usability and efficiency metrics. The consistency between high usability and high efficiency ratings suggests that the EmberGym system's design effectively translates user-friendly interfaces into efficient transaction processing—a key indicator of overall system quality and performance success.

Conclusions

Based on the analysis of data collected from 15 Grade 11 ICT students at the University of Makati, the following conclusions are drawn:

   1. The EmberGym website demonstrates very high usability. With an overall rating of 4.24, the system meets international ISO/IEC 25010 quality standards. Interaction Quality (4.44) and Functional Stability (4.36) represent particular strengths, indicating that Grade 11 ICT students, even as first-time users, found the system intuitive and straightforward. The system successfully achieved its goal of providing an accessible platform for student users.

   2. The EmberGym booking system exhibits very high efficiency. With an overall rating of 4.21, the booking process demonstrates strong performance across all efficiency dimensions. Convenience (4.33) emerged as a particular strength, showing that students could complete bookings quickly and integrate them into their routines without significant friction or cognitive load.

   3. System strengths in usability directly support efficiency. The parallel very high ratings for both usability and efficiency dimensions suggest that the system's well-designed interface and reliable functionality enable efficient task completion. This finding validates the importance of foundational usability principles in transaction-oriented systems.

   4. Specific optimization opportunities exist. The Flexibility dimension (4.00) and qualitative feedback regarding AI Bot response speed and cross-device inconsistencies identify concrete areas where targeted improvements could further enhance the student experience.

   5. The EmberGym system shows strong potential for adoption and continued success. The very high usability and efficiency ratings, combined with positive qualitative feedback, indicate that the system meets the needs and expectations of the Grade 11 ICT student population and is well-positioned for continued use and expansion.

This research concludes that the EmberGym website and booking system demonstrate very high levels of both usability and efficiency when evaluated by Grade 11 ICT students at the University of Makati. The overall usability rating of 4.24 (Very High) and overall booking efficiency rating of 4.21 (Very High) provide quantitative evidence that the system successfully achieves its design objectives of being user-friendly and operationally efficient.
