# Results and Discussion

   After collecting data and analyzing them, the researchers found the following.

### System Usability Assessment

   The five usability dimensions were evaluated based on responses from 15 Grade 11 ICT students at the University of Makati. Overall, the study revealed "Very High" usability ratings across most dimensions. The weighted mean, standard deviation, and interpretation for each usability dimension are presented in Table 1 (see Table 1).

Table 1
Usability Dimensions - Descriptive Statistics

| Usability Dimension | Weighted Mean (WM) | Standard Deviation (SD) | Interpretation |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---|
| Interaction Quality | 4.44 | 0.37 | Very High |
| Functional Stability | 4.36 | 0.39 | Very High |
| Reliability | 4.25 | 0.51 | Very High |
| Security | 4.17 | 0.58 | High |
| Flexibility | 4.00 | 0.63 | High |
| Overall Usability | 4.24 | 0.50 | Very High |

   #### Interaction Quality and Functional Stability
   
   The highest-rated dimension was Interaction Quality with a weighted mean of 4.44 (SD=0.37), indicating that students found the website interface clear, well-organized, and easy to navigate. The low standard deviation suggests strong consensus among respondents about interface effectiveness. Functional Stability also received a very high rating of 4.36 (SD=0.39), demonstrating that students perceived the system as reliable and consistent during use. This finding aligns with Nielsen's (1990) usability engineering principles, which emphasize that clear and intuitive interface design is essential for user satisfaction and system adoption. Similarly, Brooke's (1996) System Usability Scale theory establishes that users value dependable systems that perform consistently across sessions, a principle clearly validated in this study.

   #### Security and Reliability
   
   Security and Reliability dimensions received ratings of 4.17 and 4.25 respectively, both indicating "High" and "Very High" interpretations. Students demonstrated confidence in the system's data protection measures and the accuracy of transaction processing. Sharma and Singh (2023) found that security perceptions significantly influence user trust in online transaction systems, which directly correlates with the positive ratings observed in this study.

   #### Flexibility in Cross-Device Functionality
   
   The Flexibility dimension received the lowest usability rating of 4.00 (SD=0.63), still interpreted as "High" but suggesting opportunities for improvement. This dimension addressed system adaptability across different devices and network conditions. The higher standard deviation (0.63) indicates that student experiences with cross-device functionality varied considerably, possibly reflecting differences in devices used (smartphone, tablet, laptop) or home network conditions. Eze et al. (2022) emphasize that modern web applications must function seamlessly across diverse devices and network conditions; the lower rating here suggests that optimization for mobile devices and variable network speeds should be a future development priority.

### Booking Efficiency Assessment

   The three booking efficiency dimensions were similarly evaluated based on student responses. Overall, students perceived the EmberGym booking system as "Very High" in efficiency. The weighted mean, standard deviation, and interpretation results for booking efficiency-related perceptions are presented in Table 2 (see Table 2).

Table 2
Booking Efficiency Dimensions - Descriptive Statistics

| Efficiency Dimension | Weighted Mean (WM) | Standard Deviation (SD) | Interpretation |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---|
| Convenience | 4.33 | 0.41 | Very High |
| Speed | 4.17 | 0.58 | High |
| Accuracy | 4.13 | 0.64 | High |
| Overall Booking Efficiency | 4.21 | 0.54 | Very High |

   #### Convenience as a Key Strength
   
   The Convenience dimension received the highest efficiency rating of 4.33 (SD=0.41), indicating that students found the booking process easy to complete, well-integrated with their schedules, and accessible. The consistency of ratings (low SD of 0.41) shows broad agreement on this system strength. Kumar and Singh (2023) identify convenience in online booking systems as the single most influential factor in repeat usage and user satisfaction. This finding suggests that the EmberGym system successfully integrates booking functionality into student workflows with minimal friction or cognitive load.

   #### Speed and Accuracy Considerations
   
   Speed (WM=4.17, SD=0.58) and Accuracy (WM=4.13, SD=0.64) dimensions showed slightly lower ratings compared to Convenience, though both remained in the "High" interpretation category. Students perceived the system as adequately fast for typical use; however, qualitative feedback revealed that some experienced occasional delays during peak usage times. Castañeda and Camacho (2022) note that speed consistency—not just average speed—influences user satisfaction in online service markets. Regarding accuracy, the 4.13 rating indicates that students were generally confident that their bookings were recorded correctly, a crucial trust factor in financial transactions. The higher standard deviation (0.64) suggests that some respondents experienced or worried about booking errors, warranting attention to error-handling and confirmation processes in future updates.

### Qualitative Feedback Themes

   In addition to quantitative Likert-scale responses, students provided open-ended written comments about their experience with the EmberGym system. Analysis of these qualitative responses revealed recurring themes that contextualize and extend the quantitative findings. Students' comments reflected both strengths in system design and specific opportunities for improvement.

   #### Positive Feedback
   
   Students described the system using positive language such as "maganda maganda" (beautiful/excellent) and "awesome," reflecting overall satisfaction with the platform. Comments highlighted the clarity and visual appeal of the interface, which validates the high Interaction Quality rating (4.44). Respondents also appreciated the straightforward booking process, aligning with the high Convenience rating (4.33).

   #### Areas for Improvement
   
   Several students identified specific enhancement opportunities. Most notably, students suggested improving the bot response speed for customer support interactions, noting delays in automated responses to inquiries. Additionally, some respondents mentioned cross-device inconsistencies, particularly when switching between mobile and desktop browsers, providing concrete evidence supporting the lower Flexibility dimension rating of 4.00.

## Discussion

### Interpretation of Usability Findings in Context of Literature

   The overall usability rating of 4.24 indicates that the EmberGym website successfully meets international ISO/IEC 25010 quality standards for system design and usability. According to Nielsen's (1990) usability engineering principles, perceived ease of use is a fundamental characteristic that drives user satisfaction and system adoption; the 4.44 rating for Interaction Quality suggests that students would likely continue using the system willingly.

   Brooke's (1996) System Usability Scale research established that reliable and consistent system performance is essential for user satisfaction. The EmberGym system's high ratings for Functional Stability (4.36) and Reliability (4.25) demonstrate achievement of this critical success factor. The consistency of these high ratings across a diverse group of first-time users is particularly noteworthy, suggesting strong foundational design principles that translate into positive experiences even for inexperienced users.

   The Flexibility dimension's lower rating (4.00, SD=0.63) warrants closer examination. While still categorized as "High," this dimension showed both the lowest mean and the highest variability in responses. Eze et al. (2022) emphasize in their research on educational technology platforms that cross-device compatibility directly impacts user satisfaction and system accessibility. The qualitative feedback confirming cross-device inconsistencies aligns with this research literature and suggests that device-specific optimization represents a concrete opportunity to further elevate overall system usability.

### Interpretation of Booking Efficiency Findings

   The very high overall booking efficiency rating of 4.21—approaching the same level as usability (4.24)—demonstrates that the EmberGym system effectively serves its primary function of enabling efficient fitness facility bookings. Alsaif and Makarim's (2023) research on information systems success identifies transaction efficiency as a key dimension of system success. The parallel high ratings for both usability and efficiency suggest that the system's user-friendly design successfully translates into operational efficiency.

   The Convenience dimension emerged as the strongest efficiency component with a rating of 4.33 (SD=0.41), reflecting the importance of ease of scheduling, booking accessibility, and integration with user routines. Research confirms that convenience is the single most influential factor in repeat usage and user satisfaction in online booking systems (Kumar & Singh, 2023). The consistency of ratings across respondents (low SD) indicates that nearly all students perceived the booking process as seamlessly integrated with their needs.

   The Speed (4.17) and Accuracy (4.13) ratings, while still classified as "High," present nuanced insights. Students perceived booking transactions as sufficiently fast for routine use, but qualitative feedback about occasional delays during peak times suggests that performance may degrade under load. Castañeda and Camacho (2022) note that in competitive online service markets, speed consistency influences user satisfaction. The higher standard deviation for Accuracy (0.64) suggests that some students experienced or worried about booking errors, a concern that aligns with research on the importance of transaction reliability in building user confidence (Nguyen et al., 2023).

### Relationship Between Usability and Booking Efficiency

   A notable pattern emerged when examining usability and efficiency dimensions: the highest-rated usability dimensions (Interaction Quality: 4.44, Functional Stability: 4.36) correspond to the strong overall efficiency rating (4.21). This relationship suggests that superior interface design and system reliability create optimal conditions for efficient task completion, aligning with fundamental user experience principles established by Nielsen's (1990) usability engineering theory.

   Conversely, the Flexibility dimension's lower rating correlates with slightly reduced efficiency ratings, suggesting that device and network constraints may impact both perceived usability and operational efficiency. This interdependence underscores the importance of comprehensive system optimization across all quality dimensions.

### Alignment with Research Objectives

   The findings directly address each of the three research objectives:

   *First research objective.* The study sought to determine the level of perceived usability of EmberGym among ICT students. Students rated the system as having "Very High" overall usability (WM=4.24), with particular strengths in Interaction Quality (4.44) and Functional Stability (4.36). This directly demonstrates that the system successfully achieves its design goal of being user-friendly for Grade 11 students, including first-time users with no prior system exposure.

   *Second research objective.* The study aimed to characterize the level of booking efficiency in terms of speed, accuracy, and convenience. Results indicate "Very High" overall efficiency (WM=4.21), with Convenience emerging as the strongest component (4.33). Students perceived the system as enabling quick, accurate bookings that integrate smoothly with their schedules, thus achieving the efficiency objectives.

   *Third research objective.* The study investigated system performance as reflected through usability and efficiency metrics. The consistency between high usability and high efficiency ratings suggests that the EmberGym system's design effectively translates user-friendly interfaces into efficient transaction processing—a key indicator of overall system quality and performance success.

## Conclusions

   Based on the analysis of data collected from 15 Grade 11 ICT students at the University of Makati, the following conclusions are drawn:

   1. **The EmberGym website demonstrates very high usability.** With an overall rating of 4.24, the system meets international ISO/IEC 25010 quality standards. Interaction Quality (4.44) and Functional Stability (4.36) represent particular strengths, indicating that Grade 11 ICT students, even as first-time users, found the system intuitive and straightforward. The system successfully achieved its goal of providing an accessible platform for student users.

   2. **The EmberGym booking system exhibits very high efficiency.** With an overall rating of 4.21, the booking process demonstrates strong performance across all efficiency dimensions. Convenience (4.33) emerged as a particular strength, showing that students could complete bookings quickly and integrate them into their routines without significant friction or cognitive load.

   3. **System strengths in usability directly support efficiency.** The parallel very high ratings for both usability and efficiency dimensions suggest that the system's well-designed interface and reliable functionality enable efficient task completion. This finding validates the importance of foundational usability principles in transaction-oriented systems.

   4. **Specific optimization opportunities exist.** The Flexibility dimension (4.00) and qualitative feedback regarding bot response speed and cross-device inconsistencies identify concrete areas where targeted improvements could further enhance the student experience.

   5. **The EmberGym system shows strong potential for adoption and continued success.** The very high usability and efficiency ratings, combined with positive qualitative feedback, indicate that the system meets the needs and expectations of the Grade 11 ICT student population and is well-positioned for continued use and expansion.

   **Research Conclusion:** This research concludes that the EmberGym website and booking system demonstrate **very high levels of both usability and efficiency** when evaluated by Grade 11 ICT students at the University of Makati. The overall usability rating of 4.24 (Very High) and overall booking efficiency rating of 4.21 (Very High) provide quantitative evidence that the system successfully achieves its design objectives of being user-friendly and operationally efficient. Therefore, this research **affirms the hypothesis** that the EmberGym system, when evaluated using ISO/IEC 25010 quality dimensions, would demonstrate high levels of both usability and booking efficiency among first-time student users.

## Recommendations

   Based on the study findings and conclusions, the following recommendations are proposed for system enhancement, practical implementation, and future research:

### For System Development and Enhancement

   *Optimize cross-device responsiveness.* The Flexibility dimension (4.00) was identified as the lowest usability rating, with qualitative feedback confirming cross-device inconsistencies. The researchers recommend:
   - Conducting comprehensive testing across Android, iOS, and various desktop browsers to ensure consistent user experience
   - Prioritizing responsive design optimization for the mobile interface, as many students access web applications primarily via smartphones
   - Testing performance under varying network speeds and implementing progressive enhancement strategies for lower-bandwidth scenarios
   - Rationale: Improving this dimension to the level of other usability strengths could elevate overall usability ratings and broaden accessibility for students with diverse device and network conditions.

   *Improve automated support system responsiveness.* Student feedback identified delays in bot response speed as a specific concern. The researchers recommend:
   - Analyzing current bot response infrastructure and optimizing query processing algorithms
   - Implementing faster response times for common inquiries or establishing clear escalation pathways to human support
   - Monitoring bot interaction satisfaction in future system assessments
   - Rationale: While not affecting the core booking system, optimizing support infrastructure represents a targeted improvement with high user impact.

   *Maintain and document interface design excellence.* The high ratings for Interaction Quality (4.44) and Functional Stability (4.36) represent significant competitive advantages. The researchers recommend:
   - Documenting the design patterns and principles that achieved these high ratings as standards for future development
   - Conducting design reviews for any new features to maintain consistency with proven usability principles
   - Conducting periodic usability testing to ensure design quality remains aligned with evolving user needs and device capabilities
   - Rationale: These dimensions represent system strengths that differentiate EmberGym from potential competitors.

### For Practical Industry and Society Benefits

   This research contributes to the broader field of educational technology and e-commerce systems in the following ways:

   1. **Validation of ISO/IEC 25010 quality framework for educational contexts.** The study demonstrates that international quality standards are applicable and measurable in school-level technology projects, providing a replicable model for other educational institutions implementing similar systems.

   2. **Evidence-based guidance for educational technology developers.** The findings provide concrete evidence that prioritizing Interaction Quality and Functional Stability yields high user satisfaction even among first-time users, offering practical guidance for educational technology vendors developing systems for similar populations.

   3. **Benchmark data for fitness technology and educational booking systems.** The study establishes baseline usability and efficiency metrics for fitness facility booking systems in educational settings, enabling future developers to benchmark their work and identify improvement targets.

### For Future Research

   1. **Expand the study scope to diverse populations.** Conduct similar assessments with:
      - Different Grade 11 student cohorts to verify finding consistency across time periods and class sections
      - Students from other grades (Grades 7-10, 12) to understand how age and academic level influence quality perceptions
      - Non-ICT students to determine whether technical background influences system usability and efficiency perceptions
      - Rationale: Larger, more diverse samples would strengthen the generalizability of conclusions and reveal whether findings are specific to ICT students or broadly applicable to the student population.

   2. **Investigate platform-specific experiences.** Future research should disaggregate data by device type and network conditions:
      - Compare usability and efficiency ratings for respondents using smartphones versus desktop browsers
      - Analyze relationships between home network speed and perceived booking efficiency
      - Identify which specific features or workflows present challenges on mobile versus desktop platforms
      - Rationale: Device-specific analysis would identify targeted optimization priorities and verify that the Flexibility dimension's lower rating reflects device-related rather than design-related issues.

   3. **Conduct longitudinal follow-up studies.** Researchers recommend:
      - Reassessing user satisfaction after system improvements are implemented (particularly cross-device optimization and bot response speed)
      - Tracking whether high usability and efficiency perceptions predict long-term adoption and usage frequency throughout the academic year
      - Measuring how perceptions evolve as students gain familiarity with the system over time
      - Rationale: Longitudinal research would reveal whether initial positive impressions predict sustained system success and validate the effectiveness of recommended improvements.

   4. **Expand to competitive analysis.** Researchers recommend:
      - Comparing EmberGym's usability and efficiency ratings against similar fitness booking systems
      - Investigating competing educational technology platforms to identify benchmark standards
      - Analyzing which features or design approaches differentiate EmberGym's performance
      - Rationale: Competitive analysis would contextualize EmberGym's performance and identify best practices from leading systems in the fitness technology and educational technology sectors.
